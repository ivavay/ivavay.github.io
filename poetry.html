Poetry

an artist's ode

i’ve been spending much of my day
in mindless space
thinking about the alternatives
that i’m too afraid to chase

where would you see yourself in
twenty years
the assignment asks
in twenty years, i just want to be alive and well,
if it’s not too much to ask

i wrote my magazine column
down an untraditional path
the road less taken
where practicality ends
and little experiments begin

you see, i want to be an artist
of the written and visual world
i believe that art can indeed
stitch up the holes that no one sees
and wake up the soul that hasn’t spoke

tundra

tundra echoed a need for frost
what she got instead was a dash of steamed vapor
as time passed, she saw
cracks on her skin
glaciers on the blink
ice on extinct
until she finally gave in to the rising seascape around her
a sheet of alabaster expanse she is no more

diagnosis

spring.
chartreuse green
sunlit lawns
tightly rolled jeans, barefoot
faces dipped in fresh books
tranquility sets over campus
with each can of yerba drank

summer.
golden fields
dried-up hills
energy plummets
lethargy reigns
gets progressively ever more difficult
to climb this mountain

autumn.
colors in full bloom
each changing at such rapid pace
racing to get to the next stage
my mind too follows eagerly
in a kaleidoscopic wave

winter.
a prayer for peace
to settle this firing bricolage of
ideas that grows within
creativity to some as they call it
but i was told that it is something more

day one, He spoke

day one He spoke
vapors into existence
and land from nothing
it formed

day six He created
mankind, an eden
a floral garden daydream
springs forth

yet in the middle
stands a tree of the knowledge of
good and evil
to discern sure yes
curious they wondered
fell trap

why did God create
that tree
was a question that’s always
baffled me

from then on mankind toiled
their heavenly identity
relinquished, stole
by the tree
that for once
severed the sea
the relationship tied
between the creator and Eve

without the tree
we’d still be in eden

no stories, ignorant bliss
sheer perfection at ease

glass

a light poured through the stained glass
a resurrection of memories past
a thousand prismatic pieces
come together to tell
yesterday’s as well as tomorrow’s stories

fahrenheit

a sheet of charcoal dust swept across
what once used to be
towering redwoods
cobalt blue ponds
where fawns ran wild, cedar grew old
gophers dug holes, and nature shone bold
silence now hung under a melancholy sky
a tombstone, freshly burned
wrote “140 degrees Fahrenheit”

girl writing poetry

from her little hands
coupled,
eyes gazed, palms closed

hold a screen
which sprouts in full bloom

line after line
silken diction
powerful and potent
they break through mountains

reverie

i would rather live in reverie
be pulled in by the enigma
which fascinates and invites me to unfold
than to live in exposed truth
with no room to explore

crevices

he knows the crevices of our hearts.
the irritable boulder that lies between
our current reality
and where we want to be

he reveals
when we silence ourselves
for silencing a restless heart
takes defeat

an end to ourselves
is the beginning of his work in us

it is your handiwork that sculpts me
an iteration after every setback
my final version unknown
