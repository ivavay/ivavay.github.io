7/30/2019

lily and I tried out her new skateboard. we traversed down the road down our street to catch the sunset. as we walked, we somehow got on the topic of religion. we exchanged our perspectives. she said it was eye opening to hear my uncoventional experience with christianity. i concluded that i'd rather label myself as a "follower of christ" than a christian, just because to say that you're a christian could mean many things, and it doesn't necessarily mean that you are striving to perceive the world through Christ's lens.



7/28/2019

i've been thinking about the careers project ever since David and I talked about it on Thursday. what do people struggle with when they're just beginning to explore careers? i think most people find out about the career they want to go into through hs or college clubs, or through people that they interact with. i'm aware this only pertains to people to live in resource-rich neighborhoods. what if you live in a small town in some remote region of the us where you aren't exposed to potential jobs with promising career advancements. what if you come from a family of migrant workers, or a family of bus drivers? how would you you ever get the support or the courage to try for a career that you had never even heard of, much less envision yourself in? i often think for the people that i have very little direct connections to. i want to hear their stories.

7/26/2019

i have been on a hiatus of informing myself about the various inequalities that plague this world because it is sometimes just too hard to even read about these stories. but as a person wanting to go into design, i should garner up the courage to learn more about the people who are struggling at the margins of society and try my best to understand the complexity of these issues. i want my designs to play a part in leveraging the voice of the unheard.
